## Navigate to badui.dev

Open your browser and go to **[badui.dev](https://badui.dev)**. The homepage loads with eight sections arranged in a vertical scroll flow:

| Section | What it contains |
|---|---|
| **Hero** | Headline and the primary entry points to the library and taxonomy |
| **Impact Stats** | Four research-backed statistics on the cost of bad UI |
| **Antipattern Library** | A live-filtered preview of the three most recent patterns |
| **Pattern Categories** | All 12 category cards linking to focused collections |
| **Improvement Guides** | Quick-reference WCAG and Nielsen heuristic summaries |
| **Tools & Resources** | Curated free tools for accessibility audits and contrast checking |
| **Contribute CTA** | A prompt to submit a new antipattern to the catalog |

<Tip>
  Use the anchor links in the navbar — **Library**, **Categories**, **Guides** — to jump directly to any section without scrolling the full page.
</Tip>

## Browse the antipattern library

The full interactive archive lives at **[badui.dev/library](https://badui.dev/library)**. Every published antipattern appears here.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the library">
    Click **Explore Archive** on the homepage hero, or **Library** in the top navigation bar. Both routes take you to `/library`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search by keyword">
    Use the search field at the top of the page. As you type, results update instantly. The search matches against:
    - Pattern **title** (e.g., "Invisible Close Button")
    - Pattern **excerpt** (e.g., "modal", "contrast", "paste")
    - **Category** name (e.g., "Forms", "Dark Patterns")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by impact level">
    Below the search field, find the **Impact Severity** filter panel. Click any level to narrow results:

    | Level | Meaning |
    |---|---|
    | **Critical** | Severe usability roadblock. High risk of immediate abandonment. |
    | **High** | Major friction point. Significantly degrades user satisfaction. |
    | **Medium** | Consistent annoyance. Slows down users and erodes trust. |
    | **Low** | Minor polish issue. Mostly aesthetic or edge-case friction. |
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by category">
    In the same filter panel, use **Focus Area** to select a single category (e.g., "Accessibility", "Navigation"). Category buttons populate dynamically from the patterns in the database.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Combine filters">
    Impact level and category filters stack. For example, select **Critical** + **Forms** to see only critical-severity form antipatterns. A result counter above the grid shows how many patterns match.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reset filters">
    Click the **Reset** button (appears whenever any filter is active) to clear all selections and return to the full archive.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The homepage shows the three most recently published patterns as a preview. The full archive at `/library` shows every published pattern with no limit.
</Note>

Each pattern card in the grid displays:
- A **cover image** (or category initial placeholder if no image is set)
- An **impact badge** in the top-left corner of the image
- The **category** label in amber uppercase text
- The **title** and a short **excerpt**
- A **Full Audit Report** link at the bottom of the card

Click any card to open the full case study.

## Read a full case study

Each antipattern has its own dedicated page at `/antipatterns/[slug]`. Open one by clicking a card in the library or following a direct link.

The case study page is structured as a three-part audit report:

### 01 / Observation

The first section shows the pattern in its broken and fixed states side by side:

- **Rejected State** — a description and visual placeholder showing what the antipattern looks like in the wild, with a short italic description of the broken behavior
- **Optimal Flow** — the corrected experience with a description of what a well-designed version does instead
- **User Voice** — when available, an anonymous user quote describing the frustration this pattern causes

### 02 / Justification

The second section provides the evidence that this pattern matters:

- **Commercial Risk Assessment** — a plain-language summary of the business consequences, conversion impact, or abandonment rate
- **Impact severity** and confidence level for the documented finding
- **Accessibility Check** — the specific WCAG 2.1 or 2.2 success criteria this pattern violates, listed individually
- **Heuristic Violation** — the Nielsen usability heuristics this pattern breaks, cited by name

### 03 / Implementation

The third section gives you the fix:

- **Code block** — production-ready React and Tailwind CSS that resolves the antipattern, shown in a full-width terminal-style display
- **Pro Tip** — a sticky note with a practitioner insight about the fix, covering common edge cases or accessibility nuances worth knowing

<Tip>
  The **Next Case Study** button at the bottom of every report takes you to the homepage, where you can pick another pattern. Use the browser **Back** button or the **← badui.dev / research** header link to return to the library.
</Tip>

### Reading the impact stamp

Every case study header shows a dashed-border stamp with the impact level. The four levels and their practical meanings:

- **Critical** — Users cannot complete the task. Expect high abandonment rates and support volume.
- **High** — Users complete the task but with significant frustration or error. Expect reduced conversion and trust.
- **Medium** — Users notice the problem but work around it. Expect slower task completion and lower satisfaction scores.
- **Low** — Most users won't notice unless pointed out. Affects polish and professional perception.

## Create an account

An account lets you submit new antipatterns and appear as a contributor in the catalog. Registration is free.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the registration page">
    Navigate to **[badui.dev/register](https://badui.dev/register)**, or click **Sign in** in the navbar and then **Create one →** below the login form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email and password">
    Fill in a valid email address and a password of at least 8 characters. No username is required at this step — you set your display name after confirming your email.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the form">
    Click **Create account**. The page will show a confirmation banner:

    > ✓ Check your email for a confirmation link.

    Open your inbox and click the link in the email from badui.dev to verify your address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Once verified, go to **[badui.dev/login](https://badui.dev/login)**, enter your email and password, and click **Sign in**. You'll be redirected to the homepage as a logged-in user.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If you see an error message on the registration page (for example, "User already registered"), the email address is already linked to an existing account. Go to `/login` instead.
</Note>

## Use your profile

After signing in, click your account avatar or the **Profile** link in the navbar to open your user dashboard at `/profile`.

The dashboard shows:

- Your **display name** (derived from your email prefix until you set one)
- Your **Reports** count — the number of antipatterns you have submitted
- Your **Kudos** count — recognition from other community members

### Update your public profile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open General Settings">
    The **Public Profile** tab is selected by default when you land on `/profile`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Edit your fields">
    The profile form lets you set:
    - **Username** — your unique handle visible on contributions
    - **Display name** — the name shown in your dashboard header
    - **Bio** — a short description of yourself visible to other members
    - **Website** — an optional link to your portfolio or social profile
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save** to update your profile. Changes are visible immediately to other logged-in members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Sign out

In the left sidebar of the profile page, click **Sign out** to end your session. You'll be redirected to the homepage as a guest.

<Tip>
  The **Security** and **Contributions** tabs in the profile sidebar are listed as upcoming features and are not yet active.
</Tip>
